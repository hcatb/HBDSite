Copywriting for Payments & FX

Payments copywriting that speaks to the CFO and the developer

In payments, speed and cost are table stakes. The real challenge is explaining why your specific infrastructure is better without drowning the buyer in technical details. I've written for cross-border payments companies, FX platforms, and payment orchestration providers since 2019, helping them turn complex plumbing into clear commercial narratives.

The Challenge

Your buyers speak two different languages

Your developer audience wants to know about your API, your uptime, and your documentation. Your CFO audience wants to know about reconciliation, liquidity management, and cost savings.

Most payments companies default to one or the other: pure product-speak that loses the commercial buyer, or vague "transform your payments" messaging that says nothing.

I write content that bridges both: technically credible enough to satisfy the integration team, commercially sharp enough to move the decision-maker. The goal is always the same: make your infrastructure advantage obvious to the person holding the budget.

Payments Copywriting & Strategy FAQs

Do you understand the technical side of payments?

Yes. I've written about payment orchestration, FX hedging, cross-border settlement, and API infrastructure for companies like Nium, Deaglo, and Payconiq. I can speak to both the technical integration team and the CFO, and I know the difference between what each audience needs to hear.

What's your typical turnaround?

Most blog posts and articles take 2–4 working days from brief to final draft. Whitepapers and reports are typically 2–4 weeks depending on scope and research required. I'm also happy to work to tighter deadlines when needed. Just flag it upfront.

Do you work with early-stage fintechs or only established companies?

Both. I've worked with Series A startups through to established players. Early-stage companies often benefit most from getting their positioning right before scaling marketing spend.

Can you work on an ongoing retainer basis?

Absolutely. Many of my clients retain me on a monthly basis for consistent content output. I also offer a Fractional Content Lead service for companies that need strategic oversight alongside execution.

What makes you different from a generalist copywriter?

I only write for fintech and financial services. That means I don't need a two-week onboarding to understand what payment orchestration is or why ISO 20022 matters. You get content that's technically accurate from the first draft, which saves your subject matter experts' time and gets you to publish faster.
